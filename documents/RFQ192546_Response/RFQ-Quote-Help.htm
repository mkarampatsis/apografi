Readme File - Importing Responses to RFQs

 

How To Use This File

Once you have downloaded the spreadsheet, you can open it in Microsoft Excel (version 2003 or later). Excel automatically formats the display based on the style format that you selected. The spreadsheet will consist of multiple worksheets. The sections below describe the information contained in each worksheet, and the tables in the sections describe each spreadsheet field for that worksheet. As you use the spreadsheets, note the following:

  • Entries to yellow fields are required.
  • Entries to green fields are optional.
  • Numeric, date, and URL fields for Requirements and attributes in the spreadsheet are indicated by a help note to the right of the field.

Excel will also automatically format date fields according to your user preferences into the spreadsheet.

Your spreadsheet can have multiple worksheets, depending on how the negotiation was defined (for example, if no attributes were defined, the Attributes Scoring Worksheet does not appear). Use the instructions in the table below to complete the spreadsheet. Once you have completed the spreadsheet, upload it back to the system.

Common Section View this section to see overview information about the negotiation. You do not enter any information into this section.
Header Worksheet Use this worksheet to enter your responses to buyer Requirements and any other information such as a note to the buyer.
Lines Worksheet Use this section to enter your quote information as required by the buyer.
Requirements Scoring Worksheet Use this section to view any Requirements scoring information and/or guidelines defined by the buyer.
Attributes Scoring Worksheet Use this section to view any line attribute scoring information and/or guidelines defined by the buyer.

Overview

This file contains the instructions for creating and importing quotes in RFQs using an XML spreadsheet. Spreadsheets allow suppliers to easily respond to negotiations. Spreadsheet import is very useful when dealing with large negotiations ( for example, many requirements or many lines) or complex lines (many attributes) and effectively speeds up the response process by allowing you to enter data into your spreadsheet offline and upload the entire spreadsheet in a single operation.

Common Section

The common information section appears at the top of each worksheet and contains overview information about the negotiation.

Field Title Meaning
Negotiation Title Title of the negotiation as defined by the Buyer
Document Type and Document Number Type of negotiation (Auction, RFQ, RFI) and the number assigned by the system
Close Date Date and time when the negotiation closes and quotes are no longer accepted. Note that the buyer may optionally choose to close the negotiation early.
RFQ Currency Currency defined for the negotiation by the Buyer
Quote Currency In a multi-currency negotiation, the currency you chose when you initiated your quote.
Price Precision Price precision to be applied to the quote currency
Exchange Rate In a multi-currency negotiation, the exchange rate between the negotiation currency and the quote currency, as defined by the Buyer.
Company Name of the buying company
Name Name of the buyer who created the negotiation
Phone Phone number of the Buyer
Email Email address of the Buyer
Supplier Your company name, as registered with the system.
Supplier Site Your company site

At the bottom of each worksheet, the spreadsheet download time appears.

Header Worksheet

The Header Worksheet displays information about your quote. It also contains any notes from the Buyer and displays the Requirements the Buyer defined for the negotiation.

Field Title Meaning
Quote Received Time Enter the time the quote is received if you are submitting a surrogate quote.
Quote Valid Until Enter a date after which your quote should no longer be considered active.
Reference Number Enter a number or ID by which you wish to track this quote internally within your own system.
Note to Suppliers A note defined by the Buyer for your information.
Note to Buyer Field in which you can enter a response to the Buyer's note or enter a note of your own.

Requirements Information

The Requirements information section contains questions the Buyer wishes you to answer. Your responses will be used by the Buyer when evaluating your quote. You can click the View Scoring Criteria link to view the acceptable values (if any) the Buyer has defined for a particular Requirement along with any scoring and weighting information.

Field Title Meaning
View Scoring Criteria Link to the Requirement Scoring Worksheet. This link only appears if the buyer defined scoring information for at least one Requirement and made the information viewable to suppliers.
Title The gray row displays the name of the Requirement section or the text of the Requirement itself.
Quote Value Your response to the Requirement. If the Buyer defined a target value and chose to display it, a pop-up box appears when you click in this field. If the Buyer has defined acceptable values for this field, you can display them by clicking in the field and then clicking the down arrow.

Lines Worksheet

The Lines Worksheet displays the line information defined for this negotiation. Note that some fields appear depending on whether the negotiation outcome is standard purchase order (SPO), or blanket or contract purchase agreement (BPA/CPA). The table below contains descriptions of all possible columns regardless of negotiation outcome.  

If your negotiation contains many lines, you can control the display by clicking the down arrow at the top left of the lines table. You have several options including sorting the lines by line number or using the Custom option to display specific lines.

Lines Information

Field Name Meaning
Quote Total This value is calculated by the system as you enter your responses into the spreadsheet.
Line Line number and description as entered by the Buyer
Item, Rev/Job The Item Number and Item revision of the item that the Buyer wants to purchase, or the job name.
Rank This quote's rank, as calculated by the system if the buyer chose to display the rank.
Start Price The starting quote price for one unit of the item or service being negotiated. Your quote price cannot be higher than the quote start price.
Unit The unit of measure in which the Buyer plans to buy the desired item.
Quantity The number of units the Buyer wishes to buy
Estimated Quantity The number of units the Buyer wishes to buy
Estimated Total Amount Estimated amount of business you expect to pay for this line over the life of this agreement.
Target Minimum Release

The minimum amount the Buyer wishes to release against this blanket agreement line.

Quote Price

The price for one unit of the item or services to be negotiated in your chosen currency for this RFQ. Use numeric numbers, e.g., "5.75" Omit currency signs.


This is a required field for any lines on which you wish to quote. For lines that you do not want to quote on, you can leave all the quote-related columns blank. This only applies to RFQs that do not require you to quote on all lines.


If this line has cost factors, you do not need to enter a value for this field. Instead enter the line price in the Cost Factors table (see below). The system will calculate the Quote Price based on your line price and your cost factor responses.

Quote Quantity

The number of units of the item that you will buy.


This is a required field for all lines on which you wish to quote. If this RFQ does not accept partial quantities, this field displays the Quantity value and is protected against update.

For lines that you do not want to quote on, you can leave all the quote-related columns blank. This applies only to RFQs that do not require you to quote on all lines.

Quote Minimum Release Amount Enter the minimum release amount you offer for this agreement line.

Percentage of payment to be withheld

Cost Factor Information

The Cost Factor section displays cost factor information defined by the Buyer for this line. The first row of this table will be named Line Price. This is where you should enter your per-unit price (instead of Quote Price above).  

Field Name Meaning
Name Name of the cost factor defined by the Buyer
Type Whether this is a supplier cost factor or buyer cost factor
Pricing Basis How this particular cost factor will be calculated. It can be a fixed amount, a per-unit amount, or a % of line price amount.
Target Value The amount the Buyer expects for this cost factor.
Quote Value For a supplier cost factor, enter your response. For a buyer cost factor, this field displays the value defined by the buyer and is for your information only. Buyer defined cost factors are shown if they are defined as displayable to suppliers.
Quote Price The quote value converted to a per-unit price.

Price Differential Information

Price differentials are available for rate-based temp labor lines. Price differentials are used for adjusting pay rates. Note that there may be price differentials within price breaks.

Field Name Meaning
Price Type The type of differential. This is the reason for adjusting the pay rate (for example, weekend or holiday)
Target Multiplier The value by which the normal rate is adjusted. This is the adjustment expected by the buyer.
Quote Multiplier Your response to the target multiplier.

Price Break Information

Price Breaks are only available for BPA and CPA outcome negotiations. If there are empty rows in the table, the Buyer has defined the price breaks as optional. In this case, you can enter your own price breaks in the empty rows and/or modify the Buyer defined ones.

Field Name Meaning
Agreement Release Quantity How the applicable break for a quantity is determined. Non-cumulative quantity applies only to a single release. Cumulative quantity is incremented over the life of the agreement.
Ship-To The buyer's ship-to address for which this break applies
Quantity The quantity bought
Effective-From The date this break begins
Effective-To The date this break ends
Target Price The price the buyer is expecting. This is shown only if the price break optional.
Price or Discount % Whether the Price value you enter is an actual price or discount percentage of the line price.
Price The price offered or the discount percentage of the line price.

Quantity-Based Price Tier Information

Quantity-based price tiers can be used in negotiations with any outcome. They function similarly to price breaks in BPA and CPA outcome negotiations. However, quantity-based price tiers are based solely on quantity. Quantity-based price tiers are always optional, so you can enter your own price tiers in the empty rows and/or modify the Buyer-defined price tiers.

Field Name Meaning
Minimum Quantity The minimum number of units available for this price 
Maximum Quantity The maximum number of units available for this price
Price The price per unit offered for this quantity range

Pay Item Information

Pay items are used with complex work negotiations to identify progress payments that must be made over the course of a complex work project. If the negotiation was defined such that you can modify pay items, there will be empty rows and you can enter your own pay items or modify the Buyer-defined ones.

Field Name Meaning
Pay Item The number for this pay item
Type

The point in time when this item is paid. Values are Lump Sum, Milestone, and Rate
Description Description of the pay item
Unit For a rate type pay item, the unit of measure, for example, hour
Quote Quantity Number of units for this pay item. This is also shown if this is a rate-type pay item.
Quote Price Enter the price you are offering for this pay item
Promised Date The date by which you commit to delivering the item or service
Need-By Date The date by which the buyer needs the item or service. This is only shown if you cannot modify the pay item.
Target Price The price the buyer is expecting to receive. This is only shown if you cannot modify the pay item.
Note to Suppliers An informational text note from the buyer. This is only shown if you cannot modify the pay item.

Attribute Information

Information on the line's attributes is displayed in the Attributes table. You enter your response in the quote value field. If this field is yellow, a response is required, and you must enter one. If this field is green, a response is optional.

If the Buyer defined weights and scores for this attribute, the View Scoring Criteria link and the Weighted Score columns appear.

Field Name Meaning
View Scoring Criteria

Link that takes you to the Attribute Scoring worksheet. For Multi-Attribute Weighted Scoring RFQs, you should consult the scoring information the Buyer has defined. This link only appears if the Buyer defined scoring information for at least one attribute and made the information viewable to suppliers.
Title Gray rows display the name of the attribute group. White lines display the name of the attribute.
Target Value The target value entered by the Buyer for the attribute
Quote Value Your response to the attribute. If the Buyer has defined acceptable values for this field, you can display them by clicking in the field and then clicking the down arrow.
Weighted Score This field is calculated by the system when you enter a value in the Quote Value field. This field only appears if the Buyer defined scoring criteria and made the information viewable to suppliers.
Total Weighted Score This field is calculated by the system as you enter values in the Quote Value fields. It is the sum of all the Weighted Score fields. It only appears if the Buyer defined scoring criteria and made the information viewable to suppliers.

Requirements Scoring Worksheet

The Requirements Scoring Worksheet only appears if scoring criteria was defined by the Buyer and is viewable by the supplier. All Requirements will be displayed, including ones that do not have scoring criteria. View the Requirements Scoring Worksheet to determine how the Buyer will evaluate your responses to the negotiation's Requirements.

Field Name Meaning
Title The name of the Requirement.
Acceptable Quote Values The acceptable quote values for the attribute as defined by the Buyer. For a text attribute, this will be a list of values. For numeric and date type attributes, it can be a set of numbers or one or more ranges of numbers (ranges cannot overlap).
Score For each acceptable quote value, the numeric score assigned to that value by the Buyer.
Weight or Maximum Score If weights are enabled, for each Requirement, the numeric value assigned to it by the Buyer to reflect that Requirement's importance, relative to any other Requirements; otherwise, the Maximum Score for the Requirements is displayed as defined by the Buyer.

Attributes Scoring Worksheet

The Attribute Scoring Worksheet only appears if at least one line attribute has scoring information defined and the Buyer has chosen to display scoring information to the supplier. There will be a table for each line that has an attribute for which the Buyer has entered score information. Additionally, if there is a table for a line, entries for all attributes for that line will appear, even if no scoring information was defined for a particular attribute. You can view the Attribute Scoring Worksheet to determine how the Buyer will evaluate your responses to line attributes.

Field Name Meaning
Title The name of the attribute.
Acceptable Quote Values The acceptable quote values for the attribute as defined by the Buyer. For a text attribute, this will be a list of values. For numeric and date type attributes, it can be a set of numbers or one or more ranges of numbers (ranges cannot overlap).
Score For each acceptable quote value, the numeric score assigned to that value by the Buyer.
Weight For each attribute of the line, the numeric value assigned to it by the Buyer to reflect that attribute's importance, relative to any other attributes for the line.

Uploading Your Spreadsheet

  1. Once you have completed your spreadsheet, save it to the drive/location of your choice.

  2. Return to the system. From the Create Quote: Quote by Spreadsheet page. Select XML Spreadsheet as the format of spreadsheet to be imported and use the Browse button to locate your completed spreadsheet file. Once you have located your spreadsheet file, click Import.

  3. The system will import your spreadsheet information and the validation process will occur. If the system detects no error on your spreadsheet, all of your quote values will be imported successfully. You can then modify any of your quote information online.

  4. If the system discovers errors with your spreadsheet, the spreadsheet rows on which those errors occurred will be identified, and the system will note the specific error that occurred. All rows with errors will be listed in a table, which will identify the worksheet name, spreadsheet row number, table, column title, error value and error that occurred. Common errors include:

      • File errors
        File errors occur when your spreadsheet format does not match the import format supported by the system. For example, if you import a word processing file which the system does not recognize, a file error will occur. Or if you try to import the .zip file instead of the .XML file, a file error will occur.

      • Format errors
        Format errors occur when information that you entered in your spreadsheet cannot be understood by the system. For example, if you enter alphabetic characters into fields where only numeric characters are expected, a format error will appear.

  5. If any of your spreadsheet rows contained errors, the system will ask you to make the necessary correction in your spreadsheet and try importing again. Note: If your negotiation is a large negotiation, the system uses a concurrent process to upload lines by batches. If the upload process completes unsuccessfully, the upload page indicates the last line that was successfully processed.

  6. Open your completed spreadsheet file, make changes where necessary, save the file correctly, browse to the location of the updated file, and run the import process again.

Creating Two-Stage Surrogate Quotes - for Buyers

  1. To create the surrogate quote process of Two-Stage RFQ, you need to submit technical information and commercial information separately in a technical round and a commercial round. 

  2. If you use the Quote by Spreadsheet feature when you create the surrogate quote for Two-Stage RFQ, the technical information and commercial information should be downloaded and uploaded by spreadsheet separately in the technical round and the commercial round.

  3. In the technical round, when you download the spreadsheet, the following fields and sections should be exclusive in this round:
  • In the Header Worksheet, the requirements that belong to the commercial stage should be exclusive.
  •  In the Lines worksheet, the following fields should be exclusive: 
    • Quote Total
    • Estimated Total Amount
    • Quote Price
    • Quote Minimum Release Amount
    • Progress Payment Rate (%)
    • Advance Amount
    • Recoupment Rate (%)
    • Maximum Retainage Amount
    • Retainage Rate (%) 
    • Cost Factor Information, Price Differential information, Price Break, Quantity-Based Price Tier and Pay Item
  • In the Requirement Scoring Worksheet the scoring criteria of requirement that belong to the commercial stage should be exclusive in this round
In the commercial round, when you download the spreadsheet, the fields and sections that have been excluded in the technical round should be included in this spreadsheet; you can enter and upload commercial information in this round.
